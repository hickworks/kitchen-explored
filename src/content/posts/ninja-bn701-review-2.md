## Ninja BN701 Review: Is It Worth Buying?

The Ninja BN701 Professional Plus Blender is aimed at home cooks who want reliable everyday blending without the bulk of a full kitchen system. It delivers a 1000-watt motor, three Auto-iQ preset programs, and a 72-oz Total Crushing Pitcher, along with two single-serve cups that let you blend a smoothie and take it with you.

**The Ninja BN701 is a solid mid-range blender. Its 1000-watt motor handles smoothies, soups, and crushed ice with ease, and the included single-serve cups add useful flexibility for single portions. If you need a reliable daily driver and don't require a food processor attachment, the BN701 delivers consistent results at a reasonable price.**

## Ninja BN701 Full Review

The BN701 is part of Ninja's Professional Plus line, sitting above the entry-level Pro series but below the premium kitchen system models. The 1000-watt motor is enough for most household blending tasks — smoothies, sauces, frozen drinks, and nut butters all blend smoothly without stalling.

The standout feature is the Total Crushing Pitcher, a 72-oz BPA-free container with a stacked blade assembly that crushes ice and breaks down whole fruits and vegetables efficiently. The pitcher has a pour spout and a see-through lid that stays locked during blending.

Auto-iQ adds three one-touch programs — Blend, Pulse, and Mix — each running a predetermined sequence of speeds and pauses calibrated for common tasks. These are not complex, but they work well and save you from having to monitor the blend manually. A variable speed dial lets you override the presets when you want more control.

The two included single-serve cups (18 oz and 24 oz with spout lids) are a practical addition. You can blend directly in the cup, screw on the lid, and leave — no transferring, no extra dishes.

Cleanup is straightforward. The pitcher, lids, and cups are all dishwasher safe. The blade assembly is removable for more thorough cleaning.

## Pros and Cons of the Ninja BN701

### Pros

- Capable 1000-watt motor handles everyday blending without strain
- 72-oz pitcher is large enough for family-sized batches
- Two single-serve cups included — useful for on-the-go smoothies
- Auto-iQ presets reduce guesswork for common recipes
- Compact footprint fits most counters comfortably
- BPA-free and fully dishwasher-safe components

### Cons

- No food processor attachment or bowl included
- 1000 watts is modest compared to higher-end Ninja models
- Three Auto-iQ programs is a limited selection

## Who Is the Ninja BN701 For?

The BN701 is a good fit for households that blend daily — smoothies in the morning, soups in the evening — and want a machine that is easy to clean and takes up minimal space. It is not the right choice if you need a food processor or want the extra power of the 1400–1500-watt Professional Plus Kitchen System models.

## Conclusion

The Ninja BN701 is a dependable everyday blender. The motor is sufficient for most home tasks, the included single-serve cups add real convenience, and the Auto-iQ presets make operation simple. It won't replace a dedicated food processor, but as a standalone blender it hits a practical balance of power, features, and price.
