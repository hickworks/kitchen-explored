## Overview

HexClad markets itself as the best of both worlds — stainless steel durability with nonstick convenience. All-Clad's HA1 nonstick is the industry benchmark.

## Key Differences

| Feature | HexClad | All-Clad HA1 |
|---|---|---|
| Surface | Hybrid hex | Traditional PTFE |
| Metal utensil safe | Yes | No |
| Dishwasher safe | Yes | Not recommended |
| Price | ~$130 | ~$100 |

## Verdict

HexClad is worth it if you want longevity and metal utensil use. For pure nonstick performance, All-Clad HA1 still edges it out but requires more care.
