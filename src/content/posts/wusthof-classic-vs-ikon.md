## Overview

Both knives use the same high-carbon stainless steel blade and are forged in Solingen, Germany. The differences are entirely in the handle design and comfort.

## Key Differences

| Feature | Classic | Ikon |
|---|---|---|
| Handle material | Polyoxymethylene | Grenadill wood |
| Bolster | Full | Half |
| Grip ergonomics | Traditional | Enhanced |
| Price | Lower | Higher |

## Verdict

The Classic is the gold standard workhorse knife. The Ikon's half bolster allows full blade sharpening — a meaningful upgrade for serious cooks who sharpen their knives regularly.
