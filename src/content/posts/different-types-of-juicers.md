## Centrifugal juicers

A centrifugal juicer is a type of juicer that is equipped with a high-spinning blade that has the ability to extract juice from fruits as well as vegetables. The way it carries out operations is very simple because all you have to do is feed the fruits or vegetables into the tube. After that, a spinning mesh basket that is constructed with teeth will shred the feed into smaller pieces. The centrifugal force experienced by the spinning of the blades separates the pulp and the juice. The juice can then be collected in a container.

Centrifugal juicers are generally less expensive compared to other types of juicers, this makes them a popular option for people who are on a budget and also beginner-level users. To add on top, they are also easy to use, allowing you to create your juice in a few minutes. Which saves time.

The type of juicer is great, but may not be convenient in the extraction of soft fruits or leafy green. This is because the high-spinning blades make them fly around if you are not careful with your operation. Additionally, the high-spinning blades also lead to heat and friction, which may lead to loss of flavor, enzymes and nutrients.

### **Pros**

-   Centrifugal juicers are fast and able to extract juice quickly which saves time.
-   They are also easy to operate.
-   The cost of purchasing them is affordable.
-   Its large feeding tube allows you to feed whole fruits as well as vegetables without having to cut them. This also saves energy and effort.
-   They are also very easy to clean.
-   They are versatile too.

### **Cons**

-   The juicer can be quite noisy.
-   These juicers have low nutrient retention which is caused by heat and oxidation due to the high spinning of the blades.
-   They produce low juice levels when extracting soft fruits and vegetables.

## Masticating juicers

Masticating juicers are also called slow juicers or cold presser juicers. They are called these names because they have a slow and gentle action for crushing and extracting juice from fruits and vegetables. All you have to do is put fruits or vegetables into the feeding tube. The produce is then pushed to the auger or gear which rotates slowly while at the same time crushing and pressing the produce which leads to the extraction of juice.

The end product which is juice, is separated from the pulp and then put into a separate container. The cost of purchasing this juicer is high though it is worth the price. One of the advantages of using this juicer is that you will be able to extract high-yield juice. The slow operation has low or no heat or oxidation which retains nutrients and enzymes which are useful to your health.

These juicers are suitable for creating baby food, nut milk and even pasta.

### **Pros**

-   Using the blender will enable you to have a higher juice yield.
-   The slow-speed operation does not produce heat or oxidation, in the end this preserves nutrients.
-   These juicers are also versatile.
-   They produce low noise levels which cant disrupt activities or operations.
-   Their produce has the ability to have a long shelf life because they do not experience heat or oxidation.

### **Cons**

-   These juicers consume a lot of time because they operate at slow speeds.
-   The cost of purchasing them is quite high.
-   Not suitable for a small counter space because of their large size.

## Twin-gear juicers

A twin gear is also called a triturating juicer. It is slightly similar to the masticating juicer, the only difference is that this juicer has 2 interlocking gears that are used to extract juice. These 2 gears rotate at a slow speed and crush the produce between them, and press out the produce which separates the pulp. The juicer produced has high nutrient levels and enzymes. This enhances your health significantly.

### **Pros of the Twin-gear Juicers.**

-   Twin juicers have the capability to produce a high juice yield.
-   They are also able to retain nutrients.
-   Twin-gear juicers are versatile.
-   Their produce also has a longer shelf life.
-   They are quiet during operations.
-   They also come with attachments that enable you to make other foods like pasta and more.

### **Cons**

-   These juices are quite high to purchase.
-   They consume a lot of time because of their slow operation.
-   They are not suitable for a small kitchen counter space.

## How juicers work

There are many types juicers that have there own method of extracting juice, though the genereal process for all juicers is below:

1.  The first process is to feed the produce into the juicer. Depending on the make of the juicer, fruits or vegetables can be fed into the tube or hopped. Others require you to cut the fruits or vegetables before feeding them into the tube.
2.  After feeding into the tube, the fruit or vegetable are chopped or shredded by the blades once they enter the juicer.
3.  The next step is the juicer separating the juice from the pulp. An example is when centrifugal spins at high speeds to remove pulp while masticating has a slow chew mechanism to extract juice.
4.  The next step is to collect the juice produced in a container. The pulp and fiber are not reusable hence they need to be thrown. This is the final step.
5.  Once you are done, it is highly recommended to clean the juicer.
