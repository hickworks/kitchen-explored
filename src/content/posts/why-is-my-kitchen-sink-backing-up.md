## Why is my kitchen sink backing up?

Would you like to know why your kitchen sink is backing up? If yes, then you are in reading the right guide. The sink backing up is a common problem that you are likely to face once in a while. Most people wonder why it happens because it slows down the drainage and also produces unpleasant odors. Reading this article will enable you to know the causes and also solutions that can help you solve or prevent this problem.

**A sink backing is caused by a number of factors such as a clogged drainpipe, blocked P-trap, problem with the plumbing system, tree roots and more. All these issues and more can be prevented or avoided when you are keen enough.**

## Causes of sink Backing Up

Sink backing up is caused by a variety of reasons. The following are some of the causes:

-   **Food debris –** When debris such as grease, food particles and other particles accumulate. The clog or blog the sink drains with time.
-   **Foreign objects –** Sometimes sink backing up is caused by foreign objects such as bottle caps, plastics and even utensils.
-   **Fat buildup and grease** – These 2 can cause clogging, it is in fact one of the main factors for clogging. These 2 can clog the drainage slowing down the process of drainage flow and even blocking causing backing up.
-   **Insufficient venting** – The venting system should be working properly, if not properly installed you will end up experiencing water backing up into your sink.
-   **Damaged or Misaligned pipe** – A damaged or misaligned pipe can lead to debris accumulating which ends up causing a blockage.
-   **Blocked garbage disposal** – Some people have sinks that have garbage disposal units, this can also cause clogging or jamming which leads to water backing up.
-   **Sewer line blockage –** In some cases, the main sewer line might have issues that may lead to backing up. This can also affect other sewer lines.

## Troubleshooting and Resolving the Backing Up Issue

The following are tips on how to solve this issue:

1.  **Remove debris** – The simplest and easy way to start is by removing visible debris in your sink. You can use bare hands or gloves to pull out the debris. The process does not require any complex tools.
2.  **Use of a plunger** – If the problem persists, you can acquire or use a plunger. A plunger is a tool that is well-designed to handle sinks. They function by suctioning when placed over the sink drain. Carried out by vigorously pulling and pushing down. The suction and pressure created unclogged drainage.
3.  **Use drain snake** – A drain snake is also another tool that can unclog the drainage. The tool is useful for removing stubborn blockages or clogs. It functions by inserting into the sink drainage and rotating it to break any clogs.
4.  **Baking sugar and vinegar** – This is also another great option to solve the problem. Mix baking powder with vinegar to make a mixture that you will pour down the drain. The reaction between the 2 mixtures will break and clear the clogs.
5.  **Hot water –** Hot water is also a great option; all you have to do is pour hot water into the sinkhole. The water will dissolve grease and lose the clogs. Sometimes this process requires you to repeat severally before the clog is removed.
6.  **Check garbage disposal –** If your sink has a garbage disposal, it is important to check the disposal because it can also be the cause of the backup. You can check if it has any blockages or obstructions, if any you can remove them to stop backing up.
7.  **Drains –** If you experience backing up in your multiple drains, this shows that there is a high possibility of an issue with your main sewer line. You can try to solve this situation by consulting the services of a professional.
8.  **Sink strainer** – Installing a sink strainer also known as a stopper helps trap the food particles that and lead to clogging. Once the strainer is full, you can empty it regularly.

## Using Drain Snake

A drain snake is also known as a plumbing auger, it is one of the most recommended methods to remove stubborn clogs in a backed-up sink. The following are ways of using a drain snake:

1.  **Working area** – The first step is to clear or prepare your working area by clearing the space located beneath the sink. This can be done by placing a bucket or even a container to trap any water or debris during the operation.
2.  **Insert the drain snake –** After you are done cleaning, insert the drain snake into the sinkhole and push it slowly while at the same time applying steady pressure. In case you come across any resistance during the process, rotate the handle. Rotating the handle will break up the clog.
3.  Push the drain snake back and forth, this will ensure that the clog is loosened. If your snake has a claw or hook, you will be able to pull or grab the clog out. As you carry out the operation, do not use a lot of force because it can damage the pipes.
4.  **Continue –** Continue with the process until you feel that there is no resistance or obstruction. Once you feel that there is no obstruction, it will be an indication that the clog has been removed.
5.  **Removal –** Once you are done and satisfied, you can remove the snake drain slowly while at the same time rotating the handle. This is useful because it will prevent or stop any debris from sticking to the snake drain.
6.  **Testing -** The last step is to test the drain by running hot water into the drain. Hot water helps flush out any handing debris on the drainage system. If it is not solved properly, you can repeat the process again.
