Can you use Vitamix to grind meat?

## How to Grind Meat in a Vitamix?

The following is the process of grinding meat in a blender:

1.  The first step is to cut the meat into smaller pieces. This will enable the blender to easily and properly grind the meat.
2.  The next step is to fill the blender pitcher with meat, which is supposed to be filled halfway or two-thirds full. Overfilling the blend will make the blender stain, which may damage the motor or blade.
3.  The second step will require blending in small batches. In this third step, it is recommended to use the pulse function to prevent overworking of the motor.
4.  While you are pulsing, use these stops to scrape the sides so that you are able to have a uniform blend.
5.  Check the texture of the meat, you can repeat the process if it is too coarse. It is also good to be careful to prevent over blend the meat.
6.  Once you are done grinding, you can use the meat immediately or store it in a freezer or refrigerator.
7.  After completing the operation, clean the blender. It is important because it will prevent cross-contamination with other blends.

## Safety Precautions for Grinding Meat in a Vitamix

-   Use a sharp blade: Sharp blades carry out the process effectively while dull blades may make your operations difficult and even make the blender overheat.
-   Cut meat into smaller pieces: It is recommended to cut the meat into smaller pieces that will make it very easy to grind meat. This will also prevent overloading the blender.
-   Filling: Fill half or two-thirds, overfilling the blender can make the motor overheat or even get damaged.
-   Tamper tool: A tamper is a tool that is used to push ingredients down to the blade. This helps you and also prevents accidents that can occur when your hands touch the blade when pushing ingredients.
-   Monitor the temperature: Ensure you monitor the progress closely, when heat is generated the meat can lose quality or even damage the motor.
-   Liquid: Add some liquid such as water, this will act as a lubricant to the process.
-   Speeds: Start with low speeds and increase the speed gradually until you achieve your desired consistency.

## How to store grind meat?

-   **Refrigerator**: Once you are done grinding the meat put it in a refrigerator immediately to prevent bacteria from growing quickly.
-   **Use airtight containers**: Using airtight containers will prevent the meat from drying out or absorbing odors from other foods.
-   **Label:** Labelling the containers with dates will help you keep track of how long the meat has been in the fridge.
