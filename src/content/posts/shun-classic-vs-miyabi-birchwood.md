## Overview

Shun and Miyabi represent the pinnacle of Japanese knife craftsmanship. Both use Damascus steel and are aimed at enthusiasts willing to spend $150–$300 on a single knife.

## Key Differences

| Feature | Shun Classic | Miyabi Birchwood |
|---|---|---|
| Steel layers | 68 | 101 |
| Handle | Pakkawood D-shape | Birchwood octagonal |
| Hardness (HRC) | 60–61 | 63 |
| Price | ~$160 | ~$280 |

## Verdict

Miyabi Birchwood is a work of art and holds a keener edge, but Shun Classic is easier to maintain for most home cooks. Both are extraordinary — pick based on your budget.
