**What’s the Difference Between a Blender and a Food Processor?**

On the other hand, food processors are designed to carry out more heavy-duty tasks such as chopping, grinding, and shredding a wider range of foods, including tougher ingredients like nuts and meats.

Food processors are often equipped with a range of interchangeable blades and disks for different tasks, and some models also come with additional attachments for tasks such as dough mixing and slicing. Overall, the main difference between a blender and a food processor is the type of tasks they are designed to perform and the types of foods they can handle.
