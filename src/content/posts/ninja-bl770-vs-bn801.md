## Ninja BL770 vs BN801: Which Should You Buy?

Both the Ninja BL770 and the Ninja BN801 are capable kitchen blenders, but they target slightly different needs. The BL770 is a full kitchen system with a higher-wattage motor and a food processor attachment, while the BN801 is a more focused blender with a refined set of Auto-iQ programs.

**The key difference between the Ninja BL770 and the Ninja BN801 is motor power and included accessories. The BL770 packs a 1500-watt motor and comes with a dedicated food processor bowl, making it the stronger all-in-one kitchen system. The BN801 uses a 1400-watt motor but includes more Auto-iQ preset programs and comes bundled with 24-oz single-serve cups with spout lids.**

## Ninja BL770 Review

The Ninja BL770 is a high-powered kitchen system built around a 1500-watt motor. That extra wattage allows it to handle tough blending jobs — frozen fruit, ice, whole nuts — without laboring. It comes with a 72-oz pitcher, four blade assemblies (6-blade, 4-blade, dough blade, and single-serve blade), and a built-in food processor bowl that handles chopping, mincing, and grinding.

Three speed settings (low, high, and single-serve cup) keep operation simple. Auto-iQ technology handles timed pulsing and pausing sequences so you don't have to babysit the blend. The pitcher and all removable parts are BPA-free and dishwasher safe.

### Features of the Ninja BL770

- Motor — 1500 watts
- Capacity — 72 ounces
- Blade assemblies — 4 (6-blade, 4-blade, dough, single-serve)
- Speed settings — 3 (low, high, single-serve cup)
- Auto-iQ — Yes
- Food processor bowl — Yes
- Dishwasher safe — Yes
- BPA-free — Yes
- Pulse function — Yes

### Pros of the Ninja BL770

- 1500-watt motor handles tough, fibrous ingredients with ease
- Food processor bowl expands what the system can do beyond blending
- Four blade assemblies offer real versatility
- Large 72-oz pitcher suits family-sized batches

### Cons of the Ninja BL770

- Bulkier than a standalone blender — takes up more counter space
- Can be loud at high speed

## Ninja BN801 Review

The Ninja BN801 is a powerful blender that trades the BL770's raw wattage for a cleaner, more modern package. Its 1400-watt motor is more than capable for everyday blending, and its five Auto-iQ programs — chop, extract, smoothie, mix, and ice crush — cover the most common kitchen tasks without manual speed adjustment.

The 72-oz pitcher handles big batches, and the included 24-oz single-serve cups with spout lids let you blend and go without transferring your drink. The design is more streamlined than the BL770, making it a better fit for kitchens where counter space is at a premium.

### Features of the Ninja BN801

- Motor — 1400 watts
- Capacity — 72 ounces
- Speed settings — 3
- Auto-iQ programs — 5 (chop, extract, smoothie, mix, ice crush)
- Single-serve cups — 24 oz with spout lids
- Programmable — Yes
- Pulse function — Yes
- Dishwasher safe — Yes
- BPA-free — Yes
- Voltage — 110 volts
- Dimensions — 7.38 × 9.63 × 18 in

### Pros of the Ninja BN801

- Five Auto-iQ programs eliminate guesswork
- Single-serve cups included — no extra purchase needed
- Compact enough for most kitchen counters
- 72-oz pitcher handles large batches

### Cons of the Ninja BN801

- No food processor bowl included
- Slightly less motor power than the BL770

## Similarities Between the BL770 and BN801

- **Pitcher size** — Both include a 72-oz pitcher
- **Blade material** — Both use stainless steel blades
- **Dishwasher safe** — Both models have BPA-free, dishwasher-safe components
- **Pulse function** — Both include a pulse function for controlled chopping
- **Auto-iQ** — Both feature Ninja's Auto-iQ timed blending technology

## Differences Between the BL770 and BN801

- **Motor power** — The BL770 has a 1500-watt motor; the BN801 has 1400 watts
- **Auto-iQ programs** — The BN801 has 5 preset programs; the BL770 has fewer
- **Food processor** — The BL770 includes a food processor bowl; the BN801 does not
- **Single-serve cups** — The BN801 includes 24-oz cups with spout lids; the BL770's single-serve is an attachment
- **Blade assemblies** — The BL770 includes 4 blade types; the BN801 comes with fewer options

## Conclusion

The Ninja BL770 is the better pick if you want a true kitchen system — more power, a food processor bowl, and multiple blade options in one package. The Ninja BN801 is the better fit if you primarily want a great everyday blender with a tidy footprint and convenient single-serve cups included out of the box. Both are solid performers; the right choice comes down to whether you need the food processor capability.
