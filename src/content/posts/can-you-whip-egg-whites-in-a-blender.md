## The Science of Whipping Egg Whites

Under this section, we would like you to learn the science behind whipping egg whites. Whipping egg whites is a technique that involves incorporating air into the egg whites. The leads make them increase in volume, thereby producing a light and fluffy texture. When whipping the egg whites, protein is denatured in the egg whites which ends up producing a network of protein strands that trap and retain air bubbles.

In simple words, to make you understand better, the protein molecule unfolds and stretches out when whipped. Their strands trap air bubbles which in turn stabilize their protein networks well as increase the volume of the egg whites.

Denaturing of egg whites takes place due to mechanical agitation. When an acid or salt is added, the protein network stabilizes thereby creating a stable and stronger foam. On the other hand, over-beating the egg white leads to the breakdown of the protein network. Overbeating ends up producing a watery and also causes the egg whites to lose their ability to trap air bubbled.

## How use a blender to whip egg whites

1.  The first and most important step is to select or use a clean and dry pitcher container. If it contains any trace of water or any liquid, this may affect the whipping process negatively.
2.  The second process is to separate the egg whites from the egg yolks. Then place the egg whites into the pitcher container.
3.  The next step is to turn on the blender, start with low speed and increase gradually to high.
4.  The whipping process should tasks 30 seconds, or until the egg white achieve a frothy texture.
5.  During the process, you can add salt if you wish. It is not a must. Adding little salt helps stabilize the protein network which in turn creates a stronger foam.
6.  Continue the process of blending till reach the stiff peak. Time taken will depend on your blender, power used and also time taken.
7.  Be cautious not to over-blend.
8.  You can stop the blender immediately after you have achieved your desired consistency.
9.  Once you are done, you can use the egg white or store them. Ensure to store them in a clean and dry container, to prevent any contamination which may affect the textures.

## Stages of whipping egg whites

Whipping egg whites has 4 main stages which highly depend on the amount of air is incorporated with the mixture.

The following are stages of whipping egg whites:

-   **Foamy stage**: This stage is when the small bubbles have started to form and the texture is very liquid and opaque.
-   **Soft peaks**: As you continue with the process of whipping, at this stage the egg white turns its color from opaque to white. The stage is great for creating meringues, souffles as well as other delicate desserts.
-   A**fter peak stage**: This stage enables you to add your preferred ingredients.
-   F**irm peaks**: This is a stage that allows the egg whites to maintain their shape, longer compared to soft peaks.
-   **Stiff peak**: This is the last stage or also known as the final peak. This is the stage the egg whites are stiffer when you tilt the bowl.
-   **Overbeaten**: This is also called an overdose stage which delivers unwanted results.

**Why is my egg white not fluffy?**

You will be unable to achieve a fluffy texture because of the following:

-   Temperature: To whip egg whites better, they need to be at room temperature.
-   Fat: Even a tiny quantity of fat can affect the whipping of egg whites. Ensure to use clean and dry utensils. Dirt can also affect the process.
-   Ingredients: Adding ingredients at the wrong stage can affect the whipping process. Ensure to add sugar and other ingredients at the soft peak.
-   Overbeat: An over-whipped white egg will not be fluffy.
-   Dirt: Use a clean instrument, a dirty instrument can also affect the process.
