## Ceramic cookware

### **The Benefits and Drawbacks of Investing in Ceramic Cookware**

Ceramic cookware is a type of cookware that is well-built from ceramic. The cookware is a non-metallic material that is constructed from fired clay and other materials at very high temperatures. Ceramic cookware is highly loved because they are durable and have the ability to distribute heat evenly. They are also resistant to scratches and also staining.

**Different types of ceramics and their properties**

-   **Porcelain -**This is a type of ceramic cookware that is constructed from kaolin clay. They are very popular due to their durability. Made from a mixture of kaolin and minerals that are fired under high temperatures in order to combine. The outcome is a hard and non-porous surface that is resistant to scratches and chips. This type of cookware is very suitable for people who prefer quality and have the ability to reach the price tag.
-   **Stoneware –** This is the second strongest cookware under the ceramic category. This type of cookware is made from clay and minerals. The difference between porcelain and stoneware is that stoneware is fired under very low temperatures. This intern makes it to be porous thereby making it open to scratches and chippings. They are also durable and have the ability to retain heat well. This type of cookware is perfect for people who want top quality but don’t want to strain their pockets.
-   **Earthenware** – This is weak ceramic cookware. This kind of cookware is made from clay that is not fired or mixed with minerals. This kind of cookware is prone to chipping and cracking. The cost of purchasing is relatively low. It is a great choice for people who are on a budget.
-   **Ceramic coat** - This type of cookware is built with a metal base that has a coating. The coating is resistant to heat and it is also durable. The only downside of the cookware is that it can easily chip or scratch with time.
-   **Enameled coat** – This type of ceramic cookware is well coated with porcelain enamel. This type of coating offers a smooth and non-stick surface. They are very easy to clean and also non-reactive to acidic foods.

### **Pros of ceramic cookware**

-   Ceramic cookware is non-porous, which means that they do not absorb flavors or odors during cooking operations.
-   They are easy to clean.
-   Ceramic cookware is also dishwasher safe which makes cleaning them a breeze.
-   They are also free from harmful chemicals.
-   Their nonstick nature prevents food from sticking to them.
-   They are also durable and can last for a long time especially when they are well cared for.

### **Cons of Ceramic cookware**

-   They are quite expensive though they are worth the price.
-   They are also not suitable on stovetops since they don’t have the ability to withstand high temperatures.

### **How To Choose the Right Ceramics for Your Kitchen**

Getting the right ceramic cookware is important since it will determine how efficiently you will carry out your operations. Choosing the wrong one may limit you from experiencing a convenient cooking operation.

The following are factors that you should consider when purchasing ceramic cookware:

-   **Durability** – Purchasing a good quality will enable you to have convenient cooking operations and will also last for a long time.
-   **Usage** – Choosing a cookware that will serve your needs is the most important factor. Some cookware sets are superior that others in specific areas, e.g. oven safe, serving and more.
-   **Heat distribution** – Purchase ceramic cookware that has the ability to distribute heat evenly, favoring a convenient cooking operation.
-   **Budget** – Purchase a cookware set that fits within your budget. This will save you from stress for looking for funds and also failing to purchase what you aim at.
-   **Size and shape** – The size and shape is also important, this will highly depend on the storage space as well as the purpose of use.
-   **Cleaning** \-Choose ceramic cookware that is easy to clean. A dishwasher will be even an added advantage.

### **Care and Maintenance Tips For Ceramic Cookware**

The following are ways to maintain and care for ceramic cookware:

-   Avoid using abrasives since they can scratch the cookware.
-   Use warm water to remove stubborn stains.
-   Do not put them in an oven because they are not oven safe.
-   Handwashing is the most recommended method for washing.
-   Ensure you store them is a dry and cool place.
-   Ensure they are completely dry before storing.

## Hard Anodized Cookware

Hard anodized cookware is another great cookware that has the ability to enhance your cooking operations. A hard anodized cookware is one that has passed the electrolytic process thereby making the cookware to be hard and durable. This electrolytic process also makes the cookware to be resistant to corrosion as well as scratching.

Hard anodized cookware is made from aluminum which acts as a soft meta. The aluminum metal involved in this process is non-reactive, meaning that the aluminum won’t interfere with or alter the flavor of the food being cooked. This kind of cookware is safe to cook on all stovetops.

### **Pros of Hard Anodized Cookware.**

-   This cookware is durable.
-   The non-stick surface enhances healthy living because it requires little oil or fat.
-   It also has the ability to distribute heat evenly, thereby favoring a convenient cooking operation.
-   They are also relatively affordable.

### **Cons of Hard Anodized Cookware.**

-   The cookware is not dishwasher safe.
-   They are also quite heavy.

## Differences of Ceramic vs Hard Anodized Cookware

-   **Material** - Ceramic cookware is made of kaolin clay that is fired at very high temperatures while hard anodized cookware is made of aluminum that is well treated with an electrolytic process.
-   **Durability** – Hard anodized cookware is way more durable compared to ceramic cookware.
-   **Heat** – Hard anodized cookware are very good conductors of heat; they heat up fast and evenly while ceramic is a bit slower and take a little longer to heat up.
-   **Heat retention** – Ceramic has the ability to retain heat for a long time while hard anodized cookware cant retain heat for a long.
-   **Color** - Hard anodized cookware is normally dark in color while ceramic can be of any color.
-   **Oven safe** – Ceramic cookware can be used in ovens and microwaves because they are oven safe while Hard anodized is not safe in the oven.
-   **Weight** – Hard anodized cookware is heavier than ceramic cookware.
