## What causes low water pressure in kitchen sink?

Would you like to know what causes low water pressure in the kitchen sink? ## **Causes of Low Water Pressure in the Kitchen Sink**

Low water pressure in the kitchen is caused by the following factors:

1.  **Clogged aerator**: An aerator is a small tool or device that is normally screwed at the end of the faucet. This device plays a huge role in regulating water flow. With time, debris and water minerals are deposited in the aerator which leads to clogging thereby reducing the pressure flow of water.
2.  **Corroded pipes**: Sometimes pipes that are used for a long time corrode over time. They corrode, and they affect the water flow by reducing the pressure. Corroding can even lead to leaks which also affects the water flow by reducing the pressure.
3.  **Water supply issues:** In some areas, people think it’s the faucet or the pipes though it’s the water supply company. If your water supplier supplies water with low pressure, you will end up receiving low water pressure. The other one is possibly your water supplier temporarily disrupting the water line; you may end up getting low-pressure water.
4.  **Faulty pressure regulator**: A faulty or malfunctioning regulator can affect the flow of water thereby reducing the pressure throughout your house including your sink.
5.  **Plumbing issues**: Sometimes the low pressure can be due to a damaged plumbing system.
6.  **The issue** **with the** **water heater**: Low pressure can also be caused by a problem with the hot water faucet. The hot water heater my pile up sediments over time and this may limit the flow of water.
7.  **Gravity**: Gravity is also another issue; it is considered as minor. When there is a high a high level of elevation and also distance from the municipal source, this affects the pressure flow of water which is hard to solve this issue.
8.  **High water demand:**  When water is in high demand, you will find many people using water at the same time at different places. This affects pressure depending on the distance from the municipal source and the angle of elevation.

## **How to fix low water pressure in kitchen sink**

Fixing low water pressure can be an easy or complex task depending on the person carrying out the operation and also the cause of the problem.

Here are some of the steps you can carry out to check pressure issues and fix them:

1.  **Clean the aerator:** Cleaning the faucet can solve the aerator clogged issue. All you have to do is unscrew the faucet and soak it in a solution of warm water and soap. You can also use vinegar and water to get rid of the clog. Once you are done cleaning, you can screw back the aerator. This will allow easier flow of water thereby increasing the pressure.
2.  **Check for leaks:** This is another step you can easily implement. Inspect the pipes to see whether you will see signs of pipe leaks or damage. In case you get it, you have an option of repairing the leak or replacing the pipe to restore the flow of water.
3.  **Check pressure regulator:** You can also check your regulator to ensure it is not faulty by using a pressure gauge to measure the level of water before and after using a regulator. You will have to replace it if you find it is malfunctioning.
4.  **Check the shut-off** valve: Sometimes the flow of water can be low due to a shut valve which may be partially open. It is important to check so that you can open it fully if it's partially opened or fully closed. When it is partially opened it can cause low pressure.
5.  **Contact your water supplier: In case** you try the above solutions and they don’t work, then the next process is to check or report to your water supplier so that you let them know you facing pressure issues. Sometimes the problem is not on your side but on their side.

## **Benefits adequate water pressure**

-   **Faster cleaning:** When water flow is adequate, the cleaning process of dishes is faster and more efficient thereby saving water and also time. This is great especially when you have many dishes to clean.
-   **Improved hygiene:** Adequate flow has the ability to flush away food particles as well as debris from your sink. This reduces the risk of bacterial and germ-building.
-   **Prevents clogs**: Adequate water flow reduces the chances of clogs from debris and food particles.
-   **Reduces odors**: Adequate water also reduces the chances of unpleasant odors forming in the sink because the water flows. This prevents food particles and debris from remaining stagnant and producing odor.

## **Disadvantages of low-pressure water**

-   **Insufficient cleaning:** Low water pressure can make it ineffective to clean dishes and utensils. The process of cleaning will take you longer than normal.
-   **Limited usage of appliances:** When the pressure level of water is low, using appliances such as washing machines, dishwashers and more may make it difficult to use.
-   **Water stagnation:** Water pressure can lead to stagnation of water in the pipes and fixture which may lead to the development of bacteria and microorganisms. This is harmful to one’s health.
-   **Risk of leaks**: Low pressure has the ability to create stress on pipes and also fixtures, this increases the chances of leaks being developed.

## **Tips to help prevent low water pressure**

-   Ensure that your pipes as well as fixtures are always clean.
-   Check the pressure of water regularly so that you are able to solve issues early enough before they become extreme.
-   You can also install a water pressure booster pump to help you improve the pressure of water.
-   You can also upgrade a water meter in case you are using an outdated one, this will also help you improve or increase the pressure of water.
