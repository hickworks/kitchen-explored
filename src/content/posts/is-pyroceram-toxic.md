## **IS PYROCERAM TOXIC**

What is pyroceram? Pyroceram is a glass ceramic material that has become popular in recent years due to its advantages over other materials. It's attractive, durable, and also easy to work with. There's one question that remains unanswered, Is it toxic? In this article, we'll explore the advantages as well as potential risks of using pyroceram and try to answer the question of whether or not it is toxic.

**Is Pyroceram Toxic?**

**Pyroceram is not toxic. The material is non-toxic and has a non-reactive surface thereby making it safe for use in food cooking and also storage. The material is not affected by acidic foods nor does it seep into metal or any food substances. The material does not retain odors as well as food substances.**  

## **Different Uses and Applications of Pyroceram**

**Cookware –** Pyroceram is a popular material when it comes to the manufacture of cookware instruments such as pans, saucepans, and more due to their ability to withstand high temperatures as well as resistance to thermal shock.

**Electronics –** Pyroceram has proven to be a good insulator with its ability to withstand high temperatures, for this reason, electronic devices use it as a substrate material in devices such as ovens, and electronic components as well as a semiconductor in manufacturing.

**Industrial processing** – The material can also be used in industries to manufacture glass and ceramic. Glass and ceramic are used to manufacture cookware equipment

**Medical Implants** – Pyroceram is biocompatible, which means that the material can be used when it comes to medical applications that come into contact with any living tissue. A good example of these applications includes dental restorations, artificial joints, medical implants, and more.

**Aerospace** – The material is lightweight and strong which makes it a perfect material for aerospace applications like rocket engine components, heat exchangers, nozzles, and more.

## Safety Tips When Working with Pyroceram cookware

**Avoid continuous heat exposure –** Pyroceram is heat resistant, but after continuous heat exposure for a long time, they tend to degrade.

**Utilize proper utensils** – Avoid using metal utensils because they can scratch or abrade pyroceram cookware surfaces, instead use wood, plastic, or silicone utensils.

**Allow to cool** – It is very important to allow pyroceram cookware to cool first before washing or storing it in a fridge.

**Cleaning** – When washing or cleaning, it’s very important that you avoid abrasive or steel wool because they can damage the cookware. Instead, use soap and water.

**Use oven mitts** – It is very important to use oven mitts or potholders when removing from an oven, doing it with bare hands can injure your hands because the cookware will be too hot to handle.

**Avoid stacking** – Stacking pyroceram cookware may lead to chipping or cracking.

## Potential Health Hazards of Pyroceram cookware.

In some cases, the cookware may scratch and this may lead leaking of lead and chemicals into your food, which may not be good for your health.

It is also important to note that the health hazards of pyroceram cookware are very small when you strictly follow the manufactures instructions and also maintain the cookware properly. Ensure to inspect the instrument regularly for any signs of cracking or warping.
